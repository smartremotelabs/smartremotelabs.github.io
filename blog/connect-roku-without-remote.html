How to Connect Your Roku TV Without a Physical Remote

Published: May 21, 2026 Reading time: 5 minutes

Whether you've misplaced your Roku remote or it's simply not working, controlling your Roku TV without a physical remote is easier than you might think. In this guide, we'll walk you through the simple steps to connect and control your Roku TV using your Android smartphone.

Why You Need a Roku Remote Control App

A Roku remote control app like Smart Roku Remote offers several advantages:

  • No need to buy an expensive replacement remote
  • Use your phone as an instant substitute
  • Easier text input using your phone's keyboard
  • Control from anywhere in your home
  • Works even if you've lost your physical remote

Prerequisites

Before you start, make sure you have:

  • A Roku TV or Roku streaming device
  • An Android smartphone (Android 5.0 or higher)
  • Both devices connected to the same WiFi network
  • Smart Roku Remote app installed on your phone

Step-by-Step Guide: Connecting Without a Remote

Step 1: Initial Roku Setup (If First Time)

If your Roku TV is brand new, you'll need a remote to do the initial setup. If you don't have one:

  • Check if it came with a physical remote in the box
  • Borrow a remote from a friend temporarily
  • Most Roku TVs have physical buttons to navigate setup

Step 2: Download Smart Roku Remote

On your Android phone:

  1. Open the Google Play Store
  2. Search for "Smart Roku Remote"
  3. Tap Install
  4. Wait for the app to download and install
  5. Open the app

Step 3: Connect to Your Roku Device

Once you open Smart Roku Remote:

  1. Make sure your phone and Roku are on the same WiFi network
  2. The app will automatically search for Roku devices
  3. Your Roku TV should appear in the device list
  4. Tap on your Roku device to connect
  5. A confirmation code may appear - accept the pairing

Step 4: Start Controlling Your Roku TV

Once connected, you can:

  • Navigate using the directional pad
  • Press OK to select items
  • Use the Home button to return to the main menu
  • Control volume and mute
  • Search and launch apps
  • Browse your streaming apps

Troubleshooting Connection Issues

App Won't Find Your Roku Device

Solution:

  • Restart both your phone and Roku TV
  • Verify both devices are on the same WiFi network
  • Make sure your WiFi is stable and strong
  • Move closer to your router
  • Uninstall and reinstall the app

Connection Keeps Dropping

Solution:

  • Restart your WiFi router
  • Move closer to your router
  • Check for WiFi interference from other devices
  • Update your router firmware

Controls Are Slow or Unresponsive

Solution:

  • Close other apps running in the background
  • Check your WiFi signal strength
  • Restart both devices
  • Update Smart Roku Remote to the latest version
