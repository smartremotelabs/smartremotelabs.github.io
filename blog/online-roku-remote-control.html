What Is an Online Roku Remote Control?

An "online Roku remote control" refers to any digital method of controlling your Roku streaming device without using the original physical remote. This includes smartphone apps and web-based tools that connect to your Roku over your local Wi-Fi network.

Roku devices support a protocol called the External Control Protocol (ECP), which allows other devices on the same Wi-Fi network to send commands to your Roku TV — just like a physical remote would. This is what makes third-party remote apps possible.

The key requirement: your phone and your Roku must be on the same Wi-Fi network.

💡 Important: All Roku remote apps — whether browser-based or Android apps — work over your local Wi-Fi network. This means your Roku doesn't need to be accessible from the internet; it just needs to be on the same home network as your phone.

One Extra Setting to Check on Your Roku TV

If the app can't find your Roku, check this setting on the TV:

  1. Go to Settings on your Roku home screen
  2. Select System → Advanced System Settings
  3. Select Control by Mobile Apps
  4. Set it to Permissive

Once enabled, your Roku will allow the app to connect. This is a one-time setup step.
